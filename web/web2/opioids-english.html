What are Opioids?

Opioids help control pain, breathlessness, and cough.

Available as liquid, tablets, patches, or injections.

Common types: dihydrocodeine, tramadol, morphine, fentanyl, oxycodone.

Morphine is the gold standard for cancer pain relief.

Works quickly (peaks at 1 hour oral), lasts about 4 hours.

Common Fears Answered

Do opioids speed up death?

When used correctly, opioids improve comfort and quality of life without shortening lifespan.

Will I get addicted?

Following your medical team's instructions prevents addiction.

Do they mask my condition?

Pain control doesn't hide your condition. Doctors use other signs and tests.

Wait until pain is unbearable?

Pain works best managed early, before it becomes severe.

Will effects wear off?

Doses adjust for disease changes. No maximum dose limit.

Does this mean end is near?

Opioids help people with severe pain live comfortably every day.

Side Effects & Solutions

Constipation

Prevented with laxatives given alongside opioids.

Dry mouth

Sip water, suck ice chips or candy throughout the day.

Nausea (usually temporary)

Take after meals. Usually improves quickly.

Drowsiness/Dizziness

Avoid driving or machinery until adjusted.

How to Take Safely

1

Never exceed your doctor's prescribed dose.

2

Don't double dose if you miss one.

3

For breakthrough pain: wait 1 hour between extra doses.

4

Record extra doses and tell your doctor next visit.
